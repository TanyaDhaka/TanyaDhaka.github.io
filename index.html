About

I am an aspiring Earth and environmental scientist interested in how climate variability, water availability, and ecosystem processes interact to shape vegetation and habitat resilience.

Ecohydrology • Climate-Vegetation Interactions • Remote Sensing • Environmental Data Science • Climate Change

I previously worked for five years in Semiconductor chip design at Intel Corporation, where I developed strong quantitative and computational skills through large-scale data analysis, modeling, and automation. These experiences motivated my transition toward climate and ecosystem science, where I aim to apply a rigorous technical foundation to questions of environmental change.

Research

I am broadly interested in understanding how climate variability and water limitation shape ecosystem function, vegetation dynamics, and ecological resilience across diverse biomes. In my current project, I conducted a quantitative analysis comparing vegetation drought response across two contrasting climate regimes: the monsoon-driven Western Ghats of India and the Mediterranean climate of Central California.

  • Vegetation Drought Recovery Modeling Using NDVI + VOD + Soil Moisture (India vs California)
  • ML Prediction of Vegetation Stress (VPD + Rainfall + Landcover)
  • NDVI–VOD Divergence as Ecosystem Collapse Warning Signal
  • Wildlife Habitat Compression Under Drought

About

I am an aspiring Earth and environmental scientist interested in how climate variability, water availability, and ecosystem processes interact to shape vegetation and habitat resilience. My current projects examine drought impacts across contrasting climate regimes, using satellite observations and environmental data to study vegetation stress, soil moisture dynamics, and recovery patterns. I am also developing complementary mini-projects in machine learning-based vegetation stress prediction, NDVI-VOD divergence analysis, and wildlife-habitat vulnerability under drought.

Ecohydrology • Climate-Vegetation Interactions • Remote Sensing • Environmental Data Science • Climate Change

I previously worked for five years as a Physical Design engineer at Intel Corporation, where I developed strong quantitative and computational skills through large-scale data analysis, modeling, and automation. These experiences motivated my transition toward climate and ecosystem science, where I aim to apply a rigorous technical foundation to questions of environmental change.

Research

I am broadly interested in how ecosystems respond to drought, heat, and changing water availability, combining satellite remote sensing, climate data, and predictive modeling.

  • Vegetation drought impacts, recovery, and resilience in semi\-arid and monsoon systems.
  • Integration of NDVI, VOD, soil moisture, and climate drivers (e.g., VPD, rainfall) to quantify stress.
  • Using machine learning and statistical models to identify early warning signals of ecosystem degradation.
  • Links between ecosystem change, wildlife habitat compression, and human land use.

Selected Projects

  • Vegetation Drought Recovery in India and California (2025)
    NDVI, VOD, and soil moisture analysis to compare post\-drought recovery across monsoon\- and Mediterranean\-type climates.
  • Predicting Vegetation Stress with Climate and Land Cover (2025)
    Machine learning models using VPD, rainfall, and land cover to predict spatio\-temporal patterns of vegetation stress.
  • NDVI–VOD Divergence as an Early Warning Signal (2025)
    Exploring divergence between greenness and canopy water content as a potential indicator of ecosystem tipping risk.
  • Wildlife Habitat Compression Under Drought (2025)
    Conceptual and data\-driven work on how drought concentrates wildlife into shrinking refugia.

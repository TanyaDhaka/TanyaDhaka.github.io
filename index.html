About

I am interested in how climate variability, water limitation, and extremes shape ecosystem function, vegetation dynamics, and ecological resilience.

I previously worked for five years in semiconductor chip design at Intel Corporation, where I developed strong quantitative and computational skills through large-scale data analysis, modeling, and automation. These experiences motivated my transition toward climate and ecosystem science, where I aim to apply a rigorous technical foundation to questions of environmental change.

Research

I am broadly interested in understanding how climate variability and water limitation shape ecosystem function, vegetation dynamics, and ecological resilience across diverse biomes. In my current independent research, I conducted a quantitative analysis comparing vegetation drought response across two contrasting climate regimes: the monsoon-driven Western Ghats of India and the Mediterranean climate of Central California.

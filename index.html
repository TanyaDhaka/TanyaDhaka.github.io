About

I am an aspiring Earth and environmental scientist interested in how climate variability, water availability, and ecosystem processes interact to shape vegetation and habitat resilience. My current projects examine drought impacts across contrasting climate regimes, using satellite observations and environmental data to study vegetation stress, soil moisture dynamics, and recovery patterns.

I am also developing complementary mini-projects in machine learning–based vegetation stress prediction, NDVI–VOD divergence analysis, and wildlife-habitat vulnerability under drought.

Ecohydrology • Climate–vegetation interactions • Remote sensing • Environmental data science • Climate change

I previously worked for five years in semiconductor chip design at Intel Corporation, where I developed strong quantitative and computational skills through large-scale data analysis, modeling, and automation. These experiences motivated my transition toward climate and ecosystem science, where I aim to apply a rigorous technical foundation to questions of environmental change.

Research

I am broadly interested in understanding how climate variability and water limitation shape ecosystem function, vegetation dynamics, and ecological resilience across diverse biomes. I work with satellite remote sensing, climate data, and simple models to explore how ecosystems respond to stress and recover.

  • Drought impacts on vegetation productivity, recovery timescales, and resilience.
  • Integration of NDVI, VOD, and soil moisture to characterize plant water stress.
  • Machine learning approaches to predict vegetation stress from climate drivers and land cover.
  • Conceptual links between ecosystem change, wildlife habitat compression, and human land use.

Selected Projects

  • Vegetation Drought Recovery Modeling Using NDVI, VOD, and Soil Moisture (India vs. California) (2025)
    Comparative analysis of post-drought recovery across monsoon and Mediterranean climate regimes.
  • ML Prediction of Vegetation Stress (VPD, Rainfall, Land Cover) (2025)
    Machine learning models to predict vegetation stress using atmospheric demand, precipitation, and land cover.
  • NDVI–VOD Divergence as an Ecosystem Collapse Warning Signal (2025)
    Exploring divergence between greenness and canopy water content as a potential early warning indicator.
  • Wildlife Habitat Compression Under Drought (2025)
    Conceptual and data-driven work on how drought concentrates wildlife into shrinking refugia.
  • Soil Moisture Memory Modeling (2025)
    Simple models of soil moisture persistence and its role in vegetation response to climate variability.
